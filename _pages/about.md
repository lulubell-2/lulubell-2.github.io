Welcome to my page! I am a Ph.D. student in the EMC² Lab (Efficient Mobility via Connectivity and Control) at Clemson University, where I am advised by [Dr. Ardalan Vahidi](https://cecas.clemson.edu/~avahidi/). My research focuses on automated driving, with an emphasis on developing control and prediction methods that improve vehicle energy efficiency.

Before joining Clemson, I earned my B.S. in Mechanical Engineering from the University of Illinois Urbana–Champaign and my M.S. in Robotics from the University of Michigan. After graduation, I spent nearly two years at MathWorks developing robotics applications. These experiences broadened my perspective across robotics and autonomous driving, including computer vision, motion planning, and mechanical design, and ultimately motivated me to pursue research in automated driving with a focus on energy-efficient control and intelligent prediction.

Outside of research, I enjoy painting, traveling, playing tennis, playing the violin, and exploring new restaurants.
